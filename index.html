About Me

Who I Am

I’m a Ph.D. researcher at Clarkson University focused on power systems, stochastic optimization, and energy market modeling. My work integrates load forecasting, Gaussian copula–based scenario generation, and optimization-based dispatch for systems with uncertainty.

Recently, I developed a Gaussian-copula-driven stochastic MILP for the Tok School microgrid in Tok, Alaska, in collaboration with the U.S. Department of Energy and GE Vernova, modeling coupled electric and thermal demand with CHP, PV, and battery storage to improve reliability and economic dispatch performance. I am currently working on NYISO energy market analysis, focusing on copula-based load and wind scenario generation for real-time economic dispatch.

To explore my recent work, visit my profiles on Google Scholar and LinkedIn.
