Grid Connected PV Microgrid Design with Battery Storage Backup

PSCAD-modeled 45 kW PV microgrid with 140 kWh battery storage — ROI of 22.88% with 4.37-year payback

PSCAD Simulation 45 kW PV 140 kWh BESS 22.88% ROI

Introduction

This project explores the integration of solar photovoltaic (PV) and energy storage systems to build a resilient, grid-connected microgrid. It aims to reduce dependence on fossil fuels, mitigate grid outages, and enhance energy security in institutions such as schools and small commercial complexes.

System Design

The microgrid utilizes 132 SunPower SPR-X-21-345 PV modules with a total capacity of 45 kW, and a battery bank composed of 390 batteries rated at 48V and 10Ah. The dual-mode system ensures seamless transition between solar and battery-powered operation, managed through a PSCAD-modeled smart controller for power dispatch optimization.

Figure 1. Architecture with PV, battery, converters, and load.

Converter Design

The converter system includes:

Boost Converter

Steps up PV voltage to stabilize the DC bus and reduce mismatch losses.

Buck-Boost Converter

Charges/discharges batteries based on SOC and demand profile.

Active Rectifier

Provides grid-synchronized three-phase AC power with minimal harmonic distortion.

Bode plot analysis demonstrates robust loop stability across the operating spectrum.

Figure 2. Stable frequency response for boost converter.

Battery Storage

The battery system stores up to 140 kWh of energy, supporting up to 12 hours of off-grid operation. Charging is prioritized during peak solar periods and discharging is optimized for night-time use and peak shaving. Battery degradation models were also considered to ensure long-term reliability.

Figure 3. SOC profile for a 24-hour cycle.

Simulation Results

The system's real-time performance was simulated under varying solar irradiance and load conditions. Results show:

Daytime

Solar generation meets base load and charges batteries.

Nighttime

Battery discharges to meet load demand.

Overload Conditions

Combined battery and PV output maintains supply.

Voltage and power flow graphs confirm efficient load sharing and power quality maintenance.
