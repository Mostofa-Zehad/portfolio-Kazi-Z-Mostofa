## About

I am a PhD researcher in Electrical & Computer Engineering at **Clarkson University** (Potsdam, NY), specializing in power systems, stochastic optimization, and energy market modeling. My research integrates **Gaussian copula–based scenario generation**, **probabilistic load forecasting**, and **optimization-based dispatch** for systems operating under uncertainty.

My first PhD project — completed in collaboration with the **U.S. Department of Energy** and **GE Vernova** — developed a Gaussian-copula-driven stochastic MILP for the Tok School microgrid in Tok, Alaska. The model captured correlated uncertainty across electric load, thermal load, and PV to improve resilience and economic dispatch performance for a CHP-led system in a cold-climate off-grid setting.

Currently, I am working on **NYISO energy market analysis**, building copula-based load and wind scenario generators for real-time economic dispatch. My work bridges **optimization**, **forecasting**, and **real-world grid implementation** to advance sustainable and efficient energy system design.
